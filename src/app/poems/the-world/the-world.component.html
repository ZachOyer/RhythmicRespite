The World

Upon a summer day, I met a bonny lass
A day like any other, with a cryptic crushing mass
Discovering everything that you were

You were a mighty ship, taking me to new places
Swaying in the waves and wind at rhythmic paces
Traversing the sea by powers beyond our control
Set in your ways, slicing water with your hull
But near our destination we arrived upon the rocks
Like thunder without lightning, no electricity but still a shock
Wood becoming sticks, sticks becoming splinters
A waste of elaborate work that cost several winters
I lay upon the beach, pondering my next step
Feeling like the fly left to wait upon the web
Ever encroaching loneliness comes to devour me whole
Stranded on this island where my only friend's the shoal

And you were a moutain, reaching to the sky
An immovable presence here way before I
A natural beauty that can be seen for miles
With abounding climbers discovering your trials
I was standing at your base, looking up to you
Around it or over it, but you can't go through

And you were a whirlpool, spinning me about
Not sure where to go, I only wanted out
Confused and dazed in so many ways
Asking for breathes from a watery maze
Fighting a war with a showering hiss
My opponent is you, great Charybdis
I found my way out, I'm still feeling dizzy
Aimlessly wandering, just trying to keep busy
Ignoring the wait that might stop on a whim
When the waters die down I'd still wish for a swim

And you were a bear trap, holding me snug
An inescapable cage, a pain ridden hug
The only option I saw was amputation
Dire liberation from my freedoms confiscation
A piece of myself is the price of emancipation
A burning of bridges to stop the inflamation
But I go back to the minefield to find what I've lost
I'll be not but whole, still searching at any cost
And now I'm right back to the position where I started
By a small metal trap I've been completely outsmarted

And after all the things that I saw you be
I realized that you were the world to me
A tremendous, tumultuous, terrifying earth
Whose fascinating wonders are of great worth
But the size of the globe makes it easy to forget
The insignificant lad who you'd only just met
It takes two to tango, My rule carved in stone
Only now do I realize I was dancing alone
