The Dance

As Lyle sat in his lonely room, he traded his thoughts with the fireplace
The hungry flame threw licks into the air, crackles filling the empty space
Then out the window he heard a song, a lullaby of melancholic delight
His curiosity beckoned him to the glass, but was not answered by his sight
An empty street akin to his room, populated only by his windows reflection
But the song cared not and continued on, coming from an untold direction
Called to adventure by the bodiless tune, his loneliness awaited his inevitable return
He followed it out into the street, that haunting jingle in his mind did burn
The sound then lead him far from home, that society became a distant thought
The streets turned stones that marked the path through a forest thats perilous fraught
Betwixt the trees he walked along, until he met with a towering door
The oddity of its presence dismissed by a forest blithe to ignore
He bid adieu to the woods and greeted a darkened echoing space
A place of oceanic emptiness filled only by the songs arhythmic pace
But then the lights became like day and exposed all that was around
The grand ballroom became revealed, filled to brim with sound
For him the stage was set and ready for his performance to start
Despite the lack of audience, he was eager to display his art
His feet went left and his feet went right, a waltz he had begun
Accompanied by the faint music, the ethereal song was sung
The ballroom empty from wall to wall, but laden by his delicate dance
Strings of the bass and keys of the piano serenaded his one-sided romance
But then the song began to fade, and the lights followed closed behind
He awoke inside his lonely room, trying to catch his spinning mind
There was no tune that he could hear, no lights or stage that he could see
It felt so real but wasn't so, he pondered hard, "How could this be?"
But nonetheless he felt at peace, and didn't care that it was feigned
He had adventured with his thoughts while in his room he had remained
He would remember that ballroom dance and jaunty haunting tune
He peeked outside, just in case, but only met with shining moon
