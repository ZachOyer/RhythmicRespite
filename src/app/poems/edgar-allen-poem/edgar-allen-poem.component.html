Edgar Allen Poem

On a cold, dark Thursday night
I go to a friend's with hopes for a fright
To watch some movies, so scary, so thrilling
With monsters, gore, demons, and killing
Off the lights go and on the blanket comes
On the couch and bean bag chair have we planted our bums
The film starts up
With a warm drink in my cup
Eager to begin
The terror within


Watch them hope naught for death
Yet escapes them does their final breath
The long sleep comes for us all
With movies we watch it, completely enthralled
My eyes glued to the horror
Of sanguine blood and screeching screams
The movie, its bored her
Hailing no attention to the screen
"I'm not even scared," She says irritated
"Lets watch something else," Her tone becomes jaded
"Yes please" I say, the terror etched on my face
My backseat never felt more like a killers' home base


The movie ends, not bad it would seem
Less scary right now and more in my dreams
Fear has park in the space on my face
Still not as good as A Quiet Place
the goodbyes that I say
Prepare my fleeting way
With only a car ride ahead
Yet afraid I'll end up dead


But t'was not the monsters nor the demons
From which the movies they had been seen in
That attacked my body with their piercing claws
But my brain which gave my heart a lethal pause
Leaving my body to lay upon the floor
Living, laughing, loving nevermore
