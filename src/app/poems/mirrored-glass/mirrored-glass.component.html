Mirrored Glass

Mirror mirror on the wall
A vitric mural that light does draw

As I stand there, reading in between the lines mirror
Searching the fine print that no one else read
Looking for the loophole to circumvent ourselves
When everyone else was content with the summary
The mirror allows us to see ourselves,
never forgetting to think about us and our role in the world.
The transluscency of the glass allows us to see others as well.
Our thoughts should consider both ourselves and those around us.

And at first glance of the glass, I see my mirror
Only to mistake it for the universal gateway at my finger tips
The rainbow reflection that parades any colors one could possible desire
An enticing entrance to the factual or fictional life of another
Viewed through the same lens that I watch my own
Muddling the lines between theirs and mine
Forced to pick one or the other
An ostentatious glimpse through the eye of their storm
Or melancholic meditation with my stentorious black mirror
Misguiding me to entertain the idea
That I don't need mine when I have theirs

Yet I have the chance to pick my place
Stepping back and forth in an existential dance
Aligning my reflection with where I want it to be
Amidst the picture of positive people and pleasing places
Piecing together the perfect pearlescent puzzle in the glass
And when I find the ideal distance
I become my reflection, and my reflection becomes me
Surrounded by sympathetic souls with open hearts
In a place of abundant belonging - where I'm meant to be
I'm free to traverse beyond the edges of before
While my reflection - returned to his ephemeral cage
Doomed to vanish with a couple of my steps
But he's not overwhelmed with vehement rage
Instead, I see him smile
I see Me smile
