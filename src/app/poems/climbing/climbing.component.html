Climbing

I was told to write a poem with rhyming
So there is the wall, the one meant for climbing
Whether you go for pleasure or daily exercise
Plotting the path which permits you to rise
It builds you the muscle to be built and strong
Fearing the hold that could lead you wrong
A colorful facade sprinkled with rocks
A gym meant for thin men, not for jocks
Dreading the fall, the pain could be dire
Yet up on the wall, I desire for higher
With finger jams and tiny holds
Gotta practice that high level move
Keep climbing till the day you're old
There's always some room to improve

Though I've happened upon the probable problem
Of which climbers abilities it does rob them
The inevitable injury to my body
The price to pay for being gaudy
And so I sit here, hearing of others
Convening and climbing with sisters and brothers
Watching and wishing for what I cannot do
Hoping for healing to emerge anew
And while the climbing might go away
I know my friends are here to stay
While I'm hurt I still can talk
I'll relinquish the wall for another day
I'll happily trade the world of chalk
For a mate who asks if I'm okay
