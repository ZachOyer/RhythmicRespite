The Prophecy

Today I sit here rhyming of the future
Weaving tomorrows like a doctor and suture
Of how we'll meet and how it'll go
Hopefully happy, doubtful without a row
I'll know when I see you, and when I see you I'll know
Together we'll stay to the places we'll go
To elsewhere or nowhere, with movies and books
Pretending I'll pick your personality or looks
What letters could possibly construct your name
What colors compose the photos we'll frame
We'll share many hobbies and a desire for adventures
We'll go on them even past the days we need dentures
My dreams will be of you and the times when you are there
the lack of your presence is my worst nightmare
Hoping to be that which makes you happy
Your presence will invite warm feelings to wrap me
We'll do what we can while we are young
We'll grow out of the age that makes us so dumb
We'll have a daughter and give her a name
Something simple and cute and easy to retain
We'll have our own house on Cozy Avenue
We'll have our little girl, courtesy of you
And maybe a son, we'll think that one through
We'll have it all, wanting not fancy nor new
And when its all over we'll have no regrets
We'll have done it all, even paid off our debts
And the only thing closer than us through our strife
Are the graves where we lay when we're through with this life
