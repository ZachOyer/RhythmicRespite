Glass Rose

I happened upon a clear glass rose
A sheen of green wehre up it flows
Into the petals, outward spread
Loves epitome, made of red
It mirrors, mocks, yet inspires
The core feelings and deep desires
And now I see the metaphor
The perfect tale that it doth bore
Love does not lie, or lead you on
Its clear as day, to dusk from dawn
Pure and candid to all it shows
Not unlike a clear glass rose

The crimson lips are delicate
The stalk, a bit a hypocrite
For it bears many a thorn
Beauty's way to stay forlorn
Its form implies fragility
Not crystalline stability
The rose suggest soft and subtle
The glass returns a stiff rebuttle
While its beauty can be distracting
Let not its guise become detracting
The brittle build of this bauble
Conceals the strength true of cobble
And yet again I see the tale
The narrative the rose doth veil
Love is tender and tenacious
Its stength can last throughout the ages
And through my strife and all my woes
I'll look back to my clear glass rose
